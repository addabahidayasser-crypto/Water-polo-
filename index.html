Water Polo Rules

1. Teams and Players

  • Each team has 7 players in the water (6 field + 1 goalkeeper).
  • Teams may have up to 6 substitutes.
  • Players wear colored caps: white (home), blue (away), red (goalkeeper).
  • Substitutions can be made anytime at the designated area.

2. The Pool

  • Men: 30m × 20m, Women: 25m × 20m, minimum depth: 1.8m.
  • Goals: 3m wide × 0.9m high.
  • Game ends when the ball crosses the goal line.

3. Game Duration

  • 4 quarters of 8 minutes each (stop-clock).
  • Breaks: 2 minutes between quarters, 5 minutes at halftime.
  • Overtime and penalties if tied in knockout games.

4. Start of Play

  • Begins with a swim-off from the center.
  • Players race to the ball at the referee’s whistle.

5. Ball Handling

  • Players may touch the ball with one hand only (goalkeepers can use both).
  • The ball can be passed, carried, or shot while swimming.

6. Possession & Movement

  • Maximum 30 seconds possession before taking a shot.
  • Players cannot touch the pool bottom for advantage.
  • Swimming and eggbeater kick allowed.

7. Scoring

  • A goal is scored when the ball fully crosses the goal line.
  • Each goal = 1 point.
  • Team with most goals at the end wins.

8. Fouls

  • Ordinary fouls: pushing, holding, two-hand touch. Result: free throw.
  • Exclusion fouls: brutality or preventing goal illegally. Result: excluded 20 seconds.
  • Penalty fouls: major fouls within 5 meters preventing a goal. Result: penalty shot.

9. Goalkeeper Rules

  • Can touch ball with both hands.
  • Cannot cross the halfway line.
  • Organizes defense and protects goal.

10. Game Restart

  • After goal: opposing team restarts at center.
  • Ordinary fouls: free throw to opponent.
  • Exclusion: team plays 6 vs 7 until player returns.

11. Other Key Rules

  • Shot clock: 30 seconds per possession.
  • Advantage rule: referee may let play continue if stopping benefits the fouling team.
  • Overtime: 3-minute periods in knockout games.
  • Tie-breaking: penalty shootouts if tied after overtime.

12. Equipment

  • Ball: yellow, textured for grip.
  • Caps: numbered for identification.
  • Swimsuits: tight-fitting for minimal interference.

Water polo is fast-paced, physical, and tactical. Key strategies include center forward offense, press defense, counterattacks, and man-up power plays.
