Water Polo Rules – Full Guide

1. Teams and Players

  • Each team has 7 players in the water: 6 field players + 1 goalkeeper.
  • Teams can have up to 6 substitutes.
  • Players wear caps: usually white for home, blue for away, red for goalkeepers.
  • Substitutions can be made on the fly at the designated area.

2. The Pool

  • Standard size: 30m × 20m (men) or 25m × 20m (women).
  • Minimum depth: 1.8m.
  • Goals: 3m wide × 0.9m high.
  • Game ends when the ball touches the water beyond the goal line or crosses the goal line.

3. Game Duration

  • 4 periods (quarters):
  • Each quarter lasts 8 minutes of actual play (stop-clock).
  • 2-minute breaks between quarters; 5 minutes at halftime.
  • Overtime and penalty shootouts may occur if tied in knockout games.

4. Start of Play

  • Game begins with a swim-off from the center of the pool.
  • Players race to the ball at the referee’s whistle.

5. Ball Handling

  • Players may touch the ball with one hand only.
  • Goalkeepers can use both hands.
  • The ball can be passed, shot, or carried while swimming.

6. Possession & Movement

  • Maximum of 30 seconds of possession per team before a shot must be taken.
  • Players cannot touch the bottom of the pool to gain advantage.
  • Swimming and treading water is allowed (eggbeater kick is commonly used).

7. Scoring

  • A goal is scored when the entire ball crosses the goal line.
  • Each goal counts as 1 point.
  • The team with the most goals at the end wins.

8. Fouls

  • Ordinary Fouls (Free Throws):
  • Pushing, holding, or sinking an opponent.
  • Touching the ball with two hands (except goalkeeper).
  • Ordinary fouls result in a free throw from the spot.
  • Exclusion Fouls (Major Fouls):
  • Brutality, deliberate splashing, or preventing a goal with illegal contact.
  • Player is excluded for 20 seconds (must leave the pool).
  • Penalty Fouls:
  • Major fouls in the 5-meter area that prevent a probable goal.
  • Result in a penalty shot from the 5m line.

9. Goalkeeper Rules

  • Can touch the ball with both hands.
  • Cannot cross the halfway line.
  • Protects the goal and organizes defense.

10. Game Restart

  • After a goal: team without the goal restarts with the ball from center.
  • After ordinary fouls: free throw to opponent.
  • After exclusions: team plays 6 vs 7 until player returns.

11. Other Key Rules

  • Shot clock: 30 seconds per possession.
  • Advantage rule: Referee may allow play to continue if stopping would benefit the fouling team.
  • Overtime: 3-minute periods in knockout games.
  • Tie-breaking: Penalty shootouts if still tied after overtime.

12. Equipment

  • Ball: water polo ball is yellow, textured for grip.
  • Caps: numbered for player identification.
  • Swimsuits: tight-fitting for minimal interference.

Water polo is fast-paced, physical, and tactical. Key strategies include: Center forward offense (“hole set”), Press defense, Counterattacks, and Man-up (power play) during exclusions.
